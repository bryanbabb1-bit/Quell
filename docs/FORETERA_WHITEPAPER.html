The club's match.

A members' network for golf match play — discover an opponent, play your own round, and let the app settle the score hole by hole.

Foretera turns a club's existing membership into a living matchmaking network. Players find games across handicaps; clubs get an engagement engine and a branded presence on every screen their members touch.

Category · Golf · Community · Sports tech Platforms · iOS & Android Status · Closed beta → club rollout

Executive summary

Most golf software treats the round as the product. Foretera treats the match as the product — and the club as the network that makes matches possible.

A member posts an open match — course, tees, date, and a handicap range they'll play. Another member swipes to accept. The two don't have to be in the same group, or even tee off at the same time. Each plays their own round, enters their card, and Foretera applies World Handicap System math to reveal the winner hole by hole. No money ever moves through the app.

The result is a two-sided product with a clean split of incentives: players get effortless games and a competitive record that follows them; clubs get member engagement, new-member onboarding, and a branded home inside an app their members open every weekend. This paper covers the product, the engine that settles matches fairly, the architecture that runs it at near-zero idle cost, and the club-network model that turns it into a business.

The problem

Competitive club golf runs on informal games — but the friction is real:

  • Finding a fair game is manual. Group texts, the bag room, "anyone want a game Saturday?" The right opponent at the right handicap is often a person you've never been paired with.
  • Handicaps make it awkward. Working out strokes off the right tees, on the right nine, is error-prone and a recurring source of argument.
  • You have to be in the same group. Traditional match play assumes you tee off together. Saturday rounds and men's-league pairings rarely cooperate.
  • Clubs have no engagement layer. A club's roster is its most valuable asset, yet it sits in a CRM. Pros and GMs have no lightweight tool to drive play, welcome new members, or surface activity.

Foretera removes the friction on the player side and hands the club a presence on the result.

What Foretera is

A cross-platform mobile app (iOS + Android) organized around six surfaces. The loop is deliberately short: post → accept → play → enter → reveal.

Discovery

A full-bleed, swipeable deck of open matches compatible with your handicap. Swipe right to accept, left to pass, star a creator to favorite them. Home course softly floats your club's games to the top.

Feed — the club board

A single course's heartbeat: a live "pulse" (matches this week, players active, live now), open invites looking for a game, and the day's public results. This is the club's town square.

My Matches

Every match you're in, by state — open, pending, in progress, completed. Archive finished ones; jump straight back into score entry or the reveal.

Record — your career page

A broadcast-grade record: W–L–H, win %, current and best streaks, a five-match form guide, milestone badges, rivalries, per-course form, and home-course vs global leaderboards.

Create & Challenge

Post an open match or directly challenge a specific player. Pick course and tees, date, format (Front 9 / Back 9 / 18), public or private, and the handicap range you'll play.

The Match — messages & reveal

In-app messaging (with GIFs) to coordinate, hidden score entry, then a Reanimated hole-by-hole reveal that walks the running delta to the final result.

The core loop, end to end

  1. Post. A member creates a match — course, tees, date, format, and a +/- handicap range. It posts as open.
  2. Accept. A compatible member swipes right. Both players' handicap indexes are snapshotted onto the match at acceptance, so a later change can never alter a played result.
  3. Play. Each golfer plays their own round, on their own tees, whenever it suits them.
  4. Enter. Each enters their card hole by hole. Neither side can see the other's scores — the hidden-entry lock is enforced on the server.
  5. Reveal. Once both cards are in, the engine course-adjusts handicaps, allocates strokes, and plays the match back hole by hole: Even → 1 Up → 2 & 1. Settle off-platform.
Not a betting app — by design. Foretera never touches money. Stakes are shown for context only; every settlement happens off-platform between players. This classification boundary is non-negotiable and shapes the data model and the UI throughout.

The fairness engine

The thing that makes Foretera trustworthy is that the math is correct and tamper-resistant. Two mechanisms carry that weight.

1 · World Handicap System course adjustment

A player's stored handicap is their Handicap Index. The engine never matches on raw index — it course-adjusts at determination time, per player, off the exact tee and segment each one actually played:

Course Handicap = round( Index × Slope ÷ 113 + (Course Rating − Par) )
  • Pops = the difference between the two course handicaps. The lower handicap plays off scratch; the higher receives (higher − lower) strokes.
  • Allocation follows stroke index — hardest holes first, wrapping past 18 for a second stroke — and uses each player's own tee's stroke index.
  • Nines handicap correctly: a Front 9 or Back 9 match uses that nine's rating, slope, and par with the half index.
  • Plus handicaps (e.g. +1.2) are stored negative and flow through naturally — a plus golfer's course handicap goes below zero, increasing the strokes the field receives.

The engine (api/src/lib/scoring.ts) is covered by an automated unit-test suite spanning course handicap, plus handicaps, stroke allocation, per-tee strokes, and hole-by-hole match play with closeouts — so a refactor can't silently corrupt a result.

2 · The hidden-entry lock

Neither player can see the other's entered scores until both cards are submitted — and this is enforced server-side, not in the client. The API simply will not return an opponent's card, nor compute a reveal, until both scorecards exist. Each waiting player sees only "Your card is in. Waiting on your opponent." This removes any chance of back-solving the number you need on the last hole.

Why it matters to a club: correct handicapping and a tamper-proof result are exactly what make members trust an unsupervised game. That trust is what turns a novelty into a weekly habit — and habitual play is the engagement a club is paying for.

Why this stack

Workers run at the edge with no server to keep warm; D1 is a serverless SQLite bound straight to the Worker. The whole backend bills on requests, so an idle week costs effectively nothing — the right shape for a club-by-club rollout.

One app, two stores

Expo + React Native means an iPhone member can play an Android member with zero divergence. Builds ship to TestFlight and Google Play from a single EAS pipeline.

The Foretera network — the club model

Players are the audience that uses Foretera. Clubs are the audience that funds it. The product is designed so each side gets exactly what it values.

What a player gets — free

Effortless games at a fair handicap, a competitive record that follows them anywhere, and a board that shows who's playing at their club this weekend.

What a club gets — paid

A branded presence on every member's screen, an engagement engine that drives play and welcomes new members, and a private pulse of activity for the pro and GM.

Network membership

Status — shipped (June 2026): network membership is now a real product, not a badge. A network club gets monthly champions (three crowns — Most Wins, Most Played, Best Win % — live all month, frozen at month end and pushed to the winners), a staff pulse dashboard (in-app, staff-gated: weekly engagement, an 8-week trend, new vs returning golfers, a churn watch-list of members gone quiet, and live demand), and club identity (crest upload, club color, a pinned note). The demand engine runs on prospect boards: members tap "ask your pro," every tap is a per-member signal, and the claim pager shows that count back to staff. Next: full board theming + claim → checkout that auto-provisions the club.

Every course is playable, but a course can be a network club — a paying member of the Foretera network. The distinction is what creates the sales motion:

  • In-network clubs get monthly crowned champions, their crest and colors on the Feed, a members' leaderboard, and a real staff pulse dashboard (engagement, churn, demand). Their games surface first to their own members.
  • Out-of-network clubs still work as venues — but when a member lands on that club's board, Foretera surfaces a gentle prompt: "Prairie Highlands isn't part of the Foretera network yet. Want your club in? Ask your pro or GM to get in touch," with a one-tap share and a contact path. Members become the demand signal that sells the next club.
The flywheel: players join for the games → they create activity at their home club → an out-of-network club sees its members asking to join → the club joins to claim its branded board and pulse → in-network status pulls more of its members in. Each club's own roster is the moat; virality among strangers is not required.

Why a pro or GM says yes

Engagement

More rounds, more reasons to come out, measurable in the pulse — matches per week, active members, live games.

New-member welcome

The hardest part of joining a club is finding games. Foretera hands new members an instant pool of compatible opponents.

Brand & events

A branded board the club controls, with a clear path to club ladders, leagues, and event play built on the same engine.

Go-to-market

  1. Anchor club closed beta — prove the loop with one club's regulars (Saturday games, men's league).
  2. Club-of-record rollout — convert the anchor to a paying network club; its branded board becomes the reference sell.
  3. Member-pulled expansion — the out-of-network prompt turns members at neighboring clubs into inbound leads for the pro/GM.
  4. Network effects — cross-club challenges and regional leaderboards once multiple clubs are live.
